5 Signs Your Community
Is Ready to Build Together

If you live in Ojochal, Uvita, Dominical, or anywhere in the Costa Ballena corridor — this is for you. And probably for someone you know.

Read it. Then decide who needs to see it.

Something is forming in this region. Most people can feel it — they just do not have the language for it yet. These five signs are a diagnostic. If you recognize more than three, your community is not just ready to build something together. It is asking for it.

1
The warmth is real, but the economic distance is growing
Social events are genuine. Markets are shared. There is actual goodwill between expats and locals. But underneath that warmth, two economic realities are quietly forming. Expat money circulates largely within expat networks. Local businesses serve local customers. The gap is polite and invisible — for now.
✓ Recognize this?
2
People want to collaborate but there is no structure to do it in
Conversations happen at the Feria, at coffee hours, at informal gatherings. Ideas get exchanged. Good intentions are stated. But there is no ongoing infrastructure — no shared bilingual space, no consistent facilitation, no framework for turning conversation into action. The goodwill evaporates between events.
✓ Recognize this?
3
You have watched this pattern somewhere else — and you know how it ends
You have seen what happens to beautiful regions when the economic architecture separates. Not conflict. Something quieter: two parallel lives sharing a coastline and little else. The kids grow up knowing both worlds exist but belonging fully to neither. You recognize the early signs because you have seen the late ones.
✓ Recognize this?
4
The moment to act is early — and you can feel that moment is now
Trust still exists. Patterns are not yet hardened. The people who care most about this region are still present, accessible, and open. There is a window — always shorter than it appears — where deliberate intervention actually works. The communities that build durable integration do it now, not later.
✓ Recognize this?
5
You are not willing to just watch it happen
You did not come here to witness. You came here to contribute to something. You are the kind of person who, when you see a problem forming, starts thinking about what it would take to address it seriously. Not performative charity. Not a feel-good event. Something structural. Something that actually works.
✓ Recognize this?
